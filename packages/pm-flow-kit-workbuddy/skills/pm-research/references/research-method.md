# Research Method

## Research Order

1. Confirm what question research must answer for this requirement.
2. Identify applicable knowledge-base sources and explicitly exclude non-applicable sources.
3. Summarize confirmed opportunity, user, market, benchmark, and metric context.
4. Separate facts, assumptions, open questions, and source conflicts.
5. Record how the research changes boundary, artifact routing, PRD level, or risk.

## Minimum Coverage

- Opportunity: why this matters now and what outcome should change.
- User: affected roles, scenario evidence, and pain.
- Market or benchmark: competitor, internal historical solution, or explicit non-applicability.
- Metrics: primary success lens, inputs, guardrails, or non-applicability.
- Constraints: glossary, roles, surfaces, architecture, data, permission, compliance, and operations.
- Risks: evidence gaps, source conflicts, and assumptions that affect downstream artifacts.

## Non-Applicability

Use non-applicability only with a reason. For example, an internal bug fix may not need market research, but still needs user, metric, and operational context.
