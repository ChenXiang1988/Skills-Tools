# Boundary Method

## Boundary Order

1. Restate the problem and expected outcome.
2. Lock in-scope users, surfaces, functions, states, and data.
3. Lock non-scope and deferred items.
4. Map roles, responsibilities, permissions, and system ownership.
5. List constraints, dependencies, assumptions, risks, and open questions.
6. Record decisions required before PRD or prototype work.

## Minimum Coverage

- Scope: what will be delivered and what behavior changes.
- Non-scope: what is explicitly excluded and commonly confused items.
- Roles: actor, owner, approver, operator, reviewer, and affected user when applicable.
- Surfaces: client surface, backend/system ownership, integrations, and boundaries.
- Constraints: source rules, glossary, data, permission, compliance, operational limits.
- Risks: assumptions, dependencies, unresolved questions, and owner decisions.

## Quality Bar

The boundary is ready when a PRD author can tell what to include, what to exclude, who is affected, which systems are touched, and which risks or assumptions must remain visible.
